Hi, I'm Zhijing Wu, a passionate and driven dual-degree student pursuing Applied Mathematics with a focus on Mathematics at the University of Birmingham (UK) and Jinan University (China). Currently in my final year, I’m working on projects that bridge the gap between theoretical mathematics and real-world applications, especially in areas like compressed sensing, natural language processing (NLP), and machine learning.

I’m particularly fascinated by machine learning algorithms and how they can be used to optimize real-world systems. My recent research has focused on:

Compressed Sensing: I’ve optimized Newton-Step-Based Iterative Hard Thresholding algorithms, significantly improving their speed and efficiency.
NLP & LoRA: I’m exploring how Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) can improve language model performance with reduced computational costs.
Climate Modeling: I’ve worked on simulating physical climate models using transformer-based architectures, surpassing traditional methods in accuracy.
These experiences reflect my deep interest in making advancements in both theoretical and applied mathematics, with a particular focus on AI, machine learning, and data science.

My research interest includes neural machine translation and computer vision.
